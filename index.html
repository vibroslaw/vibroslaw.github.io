Memory. Witness. Conscience.

Piotr Lichwała (Vibrosław) creates original audiovisual works shaped by narrative music, cinematic language, and historical pressure — developed for attentive audiences, institutional presentation, and a lasting afterimage of reflection.

These are not works about a past safely closed in time. They are works about what memory, witness, and conscience continue to ask of us now.

Why This Exists

A structured response to fading memory

This work exists because memory without structure fades — and history without interpretation becomes noise.

Why this exists

These projects are not designed as content alone. They are designed as forms of attention. Their purpose is to restore weight, context, and moral tension to histories too often reduced to summary, symbol, or slogan. The aim is not reconstruction for its own sake, but a renewed encounter with witness, consequence, and the question of what the past continues to demand of the present.

Core Worlds

Two primary narrative paths

One more institutional and immersive. One more episodic and public-facing. Both belong to a coherent authorial world shaped by history, ethical tension, and cinematic storytelling.
Institutional Audiovisual Framework Long-form

Rap-Ort

An audiovisual narrative world built around witness, historical pressure, and the consequences of silence under totalitarian systems. Its current central structure includes Prawda Sumienia as the central Polish-language work and Conscience Report as a separate English-language work developed in parallel.
