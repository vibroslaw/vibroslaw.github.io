Why This Exists

A structured response to fading memory

This work exists because memory without structure fades — and history without interpretation becomes noise.

Why this exists

These projects are not designed as content alone. They are designed as forms of attention. They attempt to restore weight, context, and moral pressure to histories that are too often reduced to summary, symbol, or slogan. The aim is not reconstruction for its own sake, but a renewed encounter with witness, consequence, and the demands the past still places on the present.

About

Work at the intersection of memory, history, and responsibility

An authorial practice developed through music, image, and audiovisual form — built not for noise, but for attention.

Piotr Lichwała (Vibrosław)

Piotr Lichwała (Vibrosław) creates original audiovisual works shaped by memory, witness, and the moral tension of history. His practice combines narrative music, cinematic image, and short- or long-form structures developed for attentive viewing — both public and institutional.

Why these forms

The work unfolds at the intersection of art, history, and responsibility in the telling of the past. Projects such as Rap-Ort and SZTAB do not aim only to reconstruct events, but to restore their weight, human scale, and contemporary resonance.
