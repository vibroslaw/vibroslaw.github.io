Cinematic Historical Series

SZTAB

Episodes of resistance, witness, courage, and memory.

SZTAB is a developing series of cinematic historical works built for wider audiences without abandoning seriousness. Each entry focuses on a figure, event, or moral turning point — combining music, image, and narrative force in a form designed for digital reach and long-term cultural weight.

Series Orientation

History does not survive by itself. Someone has to carry it forward.

SZTAB was conceived as a more public-facing counterpart to Rap-Ort: dynamic, episodic, and accessible — yet still anchored in seriousness, memory, and historical responsibility.

Foundational Principles

What defines SZTAB

SZTAB expands reach, but not at the cost of seriousness. Its ambition is accessibility without simplification.

Reach

The series is designed to speak to wider digital audiences while retaining historical weight and narrative coherence.

Focus

Each episode centers on a figure, event, or act of resistance rather than trying to contain an entire historical field at once.

Respect

Even in a more dynamic form, the treatment of history remains restrained, serious, and conscious of ethical responsibility.
